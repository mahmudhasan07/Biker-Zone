What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

  1. Helmet is older than 5 years.
  2. The interiors have started to come off.
  3. The exterior is showing signs of wear & tear.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

Before going on a long ride, take your bike around the block and make sure it is safe to ride.

  • First of all, your tires are going to be deflated, it is normal after long periods of time on storage. Inflate them close to the recommended level, you can find that in the wall of the tire.
  • Make sure your brakes work. First do it off your bike, make sure the brake pads make contact with the rim or the rotor appropriately. Then do it with while riding your bike around the block. Make sure you test it with enough speed, but do it safely. It is common for cables to stretch and for air to build up in hydraulic brakes.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike

  • PROTECT YOUR HEAD
    Our head is one of the most sensitive and critical parts of the body and any injury in this particular area can be life-threatening. Therefore, make sure that you wear a helmet and other safety gear before going out on your bicycle. Wear knee caps and biking gloves as well. Protective eye-wear can also be used to safeguard your eyes from the wind and the dust which can hamper with your visibility. A sturdy helmet with a belted fixture is essential for all cycling enthusiasts. Even if your kids are riding a bicycle at home, wearing the safety gear can help them build their habits.
  • OBEY THE TRAFFIC LIGHTS
    Traffic signals are mandated to ensure the safety provisions on the road and prevent accidents. If you are bicycling on the road, make sure that you drive on the biker’s lane only. Follow the traffic signals and do not cause trouble for the pedestrians. Bicycles are much slower than automobiles and scooters. Therefore, do not ride in the middle of the road. Respect other’s concerns and give way to the other vehicles.
